📚 Safety Guide

QR Safety Guide

Learn how QR code scams work — and how to stay protected.

Beginner

What Is QR Code Phishing?

Attackers use QR codes to hide dangerous URLs that bypass traditional security. It's called "quishing" and it's exploding in 2024–2025.

The Hidden Link Problem

A QR code encodes a URL invisibly. Your phone opens it automatically — no preview, no warning. This blind-click is exactly what attackers exploit. Traditional phishing links can be hovered to reveal the destination. QR codes offer zero preview.

📊 Stat: QR phishing attacks increased 600%+ in H2 2023. By 2024, it became the #1 method to bypass corporate email security filters.

Common Attack Scenarios

  • Fake parking meters — sticker QR codes over legitimate ones redirect to fake payment pages
  • Phishing emails — QR images bypass spam filters that can't analyze images
  • Fake delivery notices — "Scan to reschedule" leads to credential theft
  • Restaurant menus — tampered QR codes on tables with card skimming
✅ Fix: Use Scan Shield on any QR that asks you to log in or pay. Takes 3 seconds.
Intermediate

5 Warning Signs of a Scam QR Code

Five red flags that security professionals look for — plus one tool that checks them all automatically.

1. Random or Misspelled Domain

Real brands use their own domains. Watch for: amaz0n-secure.xyz, paypa1-login.support, random character strings.

2. URL Shorteners

bit.ly, tinyurl hide the real destination. Expand or verify before visiting.

3. Immediate Login Request

Legit services rarely demand credentials the moment you land from a QR code. Especially suspicious on unrecognized domains.

4. Physical Sticker Over QR

Touch the QR code in restaurants, parking meters, and public signs. A sticker on top of another surface is a strong attack signal.

5. Urgency Language

"Act now — account will close!" Legitimate companies don't deliver urgency via QR codes.

Advanced

How Scan Shield's AI Detects Threats

A look inside the three-layer analysis engine: URL inspection, live web intelligence, and GPT-4o reasoning.

Layer 1: URL Structure

Structural signals — unusual TLDs, character substitutions, excessive subdomains, abnormal URL length — analyzed before making any network request.

Layer 2: Live Threat Intelligence

Real-time search for scam reports, consumer complaints, and cybersecurity alerts about the domain. Catches newly-created phishing sites that aren't yet in static blocklists.

Layer 3: GPT-4o Synthesis

All signals are fed into GPT-4o-mini with a carefully engineered security prompt. Conservative by design — only flags danger when multiple clear signals align.

💡 Why this beats blocklists: Most phishing domains live for only 24–72 hours — too short for static lists, but long enough to steal credentials. Real-time AI catches them.
